Info

George Raymond Richard Martin was born on September 20, 1948, in Bayonne, New Jersey, USA. He developed a love for storytelling at an early age, writing and selling monster stories to neighborhood children for pennies. Martin earned a B.S. and an M.S. in Journalism from Northwestern University. In the 1970s and 1980s, Martin worked as a science fiction and fantasy writer, publishing short stories and novels. Some of his early acclaimed works include A Song for Lya and Fevre Dream. He also worked in television as a writer and producer, most notably on the CBS series The Twilight Zone and Beauty and the Beast. However, frustrated with the limitations of television storytelling, Martin returned to writing fiction. In 1996, he published A Game of Thrones, the first novel in what would become his best-known series: A Song of Ice and Fire. The books are set in the richly imagined world of Westeros and Essos and are known for their complex characters, political intrigue, and shocking plot twists. The series gained global fame after it was adapted into the HBO television series Game of Thrones (2011–2019), which brought Martin’s work to an even larger audience. Despite the TV show ending in 2019, Martin is still working on the final volumes of the book series: The Winds of Winter and A Dream of Spring. Martin has received numerous awards and honors for his writing and is often referred to as "the American Tolkien." He currently resides in Santa Fe, New Mexico, where he continues to write and manage various creative projects.
